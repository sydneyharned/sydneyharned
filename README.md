# Hi, I'm Sydney 👋

I'm a PhD candidate at NC State finishing up a degree in biology, where I've spent the last several years sequencing, assembling, and making sense of fish genomes. My work lives at the intersection of genomics, bioinformatics, and conservation — I build pipelines, assemble reference genomes, and use population genomics to understand how species respond to environmental change.

Currently open to industry roles in genomics, bioinformatics, and computational biology.

---

## What I work on

- **Genome assembly** — chromosome-scale, haplotype-resolved reference genomes for non-model organisms (two manuscripts in review)
- **Population genomics** — ddRAD-seq pipelines, SNP filtering, population structure, FST, ADMIXTURE across marine fish and sharks
- **RNA-seq & transcriptomics** — de novo transcriptome assembly, differential expression analysis, sex-biased gene discovery
- **Conservation genetics** — connectivity, relatedness, and parentage in critically endangered species across ocean basins
